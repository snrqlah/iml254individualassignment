Little Women

This book has a good flow of story. It talks about the four March sisters who have different personalities and goals.
Their story begins when they were just little girls and then continues until they become a woman who is successful
in their own little lives. Meg, the oldest wants to be loved and dreams of having her own little family. She is described
as beautiful and well-mannered. She took good care of her sisters when their mother was absent since she was the oldest.
The second sister is Jo. Jo has a strong will in her life, which is to become a writer. She often rebels herself out and has quite
a temper. But, after meeting Laurie, the lonely grandson of their neighbour, she finally understands herself. Then we have
Beth who is portrayed as quiet and known as the peacemaker among her sisters. Beth's story takes a tragic turn when she dies
from scarlet fever. Her death deeply affects her family and catalyzes personal growth and reflection for the other sisters.
The youngest one, Amy. She is known for her artistic talents. Throughout the book, her character learns important lessons
about love and family. She was also married to Laurie, who was rejected by Jo.

Siapa Sangka Terjatuh Cinta

The story revolves around Mawarti, who works as a writer. She dislikes socialising with other people
and rarely talks with her neighbours. Meanwhile, Arif is a high-spirited person who has a sweet tongue
and often chats with any random person he finds. Both of them started to know each other when Arif
accused Mawarti have boyfriends that kept coming to her house every night. Turns out, it was her
brothers. So, as a punishment, Arif needs to be the personal shopper for Mawarti's groceries. Their
feelings for each other kept growing as they shared their dark past and insecurities. They became
more than friends. What I like about this book is the amount of advice the author has put in this
book. This advice is good because I can relate to Mawarti's issue. Throughout the book, Arif has
helped Mawarti to overcome her fear of socialising and her insecurities.
